<p><p>Gaseous and liquid helium are two forms of helium, a colorless and odorless gas that is the second lightest element in the universe. Gaseous helium is the form in which helium is commonly found and is typically used in various applications such as welding, leak detection, and cooling in industries like aerospace, electronics, and healthcare. On the other hand, liquid helium is obtained by cooling gaseous helium to extremely low temperatures (-269 degrees Celsius) and is used in cryogenic applications such as cooling superconducting magnets in MRI machines, nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy, and particle accelerators.</p><p>The future outlook for the gaseous and liquid helium market is promising, with a projected growth rate of 14% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for helium in various industries, particularly in manufacturing processes that require precise temperature control and cryogenic cooling. As helium is a limited resource and its supply is dependent on natural gas reserves, the market may face challenges in terms of pricing and availability, which could restrain the growth to some extent.</p><p>However, efforts are being made to conserve helium and explore alternative sources to ensure a sustainable supply. Moreover, the increasing focus on renewable energy sources and the use of helium in fusion reactors provides additional growth opportunities for the gaseous and liquid helium market.</p><p>Overall, the gaseous and liquid helium market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by increased demand from industries and advancements in technology.
