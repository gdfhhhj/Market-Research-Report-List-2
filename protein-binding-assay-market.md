<p><ul><li>Equilibrium dialysis</li><li>Ultrafiltration</li><li>Ultracentrifugation</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Protein binding assays are important tools used in drug discovery and development to study protein-drug interactions. There are different types of protein binding assays available in the market. Equilibrium dialysis is a widely used method that separates free drug molecules from protein-bound ones using a semipermeable membrane. Ultrafiltration employs pressure to filter free drug molecules while retaining protein-bound ones. Ultracentrifugation separates protein-bound and free drug by density gradient centrifugation. Other market options may include techniques like fluorescence-based assays or surface plasmon resonance. These methods help to understand the extent and strength of protein-drug binding, aiding in drug efficacy and safety assessment.</p></p>
