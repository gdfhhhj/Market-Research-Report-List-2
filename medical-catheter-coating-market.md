<p><p>Medical catheter coating is a process in which a layer is applied to the surface of a catheter to enhance its functionality and prevent potential complications. Hydrophilic coating improves lubricity and reduces friction, allowing for smoother insertion and removal. Anticoagulant coating reduces the risk of blood clot formation within the catheter. Antimicrobial coating helps prevent infection by inhibiting the growth of bacteria on the surface. Other coatings may include drug-eluting coatings or bioactive coatings that promote tissue integration. These coatings aim to enhance patient comfort, prevent complications, and improve overall catheter performance.</p></p>
