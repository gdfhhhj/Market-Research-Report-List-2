<p><p>Industrial scratch testers are used to measure the scratch resistance of various materials. Ordinary scratch testers evaluate the material's resistance to scratches by pulling a stylus across its surface. Linear scratch testers apply a controlled force to a stylus moving in a straight line, providing more accurate results. Multi finger scratch testers use multiple styluses simultaneously to mimic real-life scenarios and assess the material's resistance to scratches from different angles. These different types of scratch testers cater to varying testing needs and provide valuable insights for industries such as automotive, electronics, and coatings.</p></p>
