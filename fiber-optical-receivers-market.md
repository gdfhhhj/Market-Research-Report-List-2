<p><p>Fiber Optical Receivers are used in high-speed data transmission over fiber optic networks. Different types of receivers are available in the market, including SFP (Small Form Factor Pluggable), SFP+ (Enhanced Small Form Factor Pluggable), QSFP/QSFP+ (Quad Small Form-Factor Pluggable), XFP (10 Gigabit Small Form Factor Pluggable), and CFP/CXP (C Form-Factor Pluggable/ CXP Form-Factor Pluggable). These receivers offer varying data rates and form factors to accommodate different network requirements. SFP and SFP+ are commonly used for lower data rates, while QSFP/QSFP+, XFP, and CFP/CXP are ideal for higher data rate applications.</p></p>
