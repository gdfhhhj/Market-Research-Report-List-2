USB 2.0 is an older version widely used for connecting peripherals such as mice and keyboards. With a maximum transfer rate of 480 Mbps, it is suitable for common tasks. USB 3.0, the newer version, offers faster data transfer speeds of up to 5 Gbps, making it ideal for high-bandwidth devices like external hard drives and cameras. In addition to these mainstream options, there are other emerging USB standards catering to specialized requirements, such as USB 3.1, USB Type-C, and Thunderbolt.</p></p>
