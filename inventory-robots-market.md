<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Inventory robots come in two main market types: mobile robotics and fixed robotics. Mobile robotics refers to robots that have the capability to move autonomously within a designated area, such as warehouses, supermarkets, or fulfillment centers. These robots are equipped with sensors and navigation systems to efficiently navigate and perform inventory-related tasks. On the other hand, fixed robotics refers to robots that are permanently installed at specific locations, such as conveyor belts or shelves, to automate inventory management processes. They are stationary and perform tasks like scanning barcodes or sorting items.</p></p>
