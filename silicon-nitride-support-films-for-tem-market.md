<p><p>Silicon nitride support films for TEM (Transmission Electron Microscopy) are used to hold samples in place and provide structural support during imaging processes. There are three types of market segments for these support films: </p><p>1. Single MicroPore TEM Windows: These support films consist of a single, well-defined pore that allows the transmission of electrons for imaging purposes.</p><p>2. Multiple TEM Windows: These support films contain several evenly distributed pores, enabling multiple samples or regions to be imaged simultaneously.</p><p>3. Microporous TEM Windows: These support films feature a large number of pores, creating a porous structure that allows for enhanced electron beam interaction with the sample and improved imaging capabilities.</p></p>
