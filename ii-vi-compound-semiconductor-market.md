<p><p>The II-VI Compound Semiconductor market consists of various types of deposition technologies used for the production of compound semiconductors. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) is a method where thin film deposition occurs through the reaction of vapor-phase chemicals. Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE) involves the deposition of atoms or molecules onto a substrate to form thin film layers. Hydride Vapor Phase Epitaxy (HVPE) utilizes a chemical reaction between hydride gases to create epitaxial layers. Metalorganic Vapor Phase Epitaxy (MOVPE) involves the reaction of metalorganic compounds to form thin films. Liquid Phase Epitaxy (LPE) uses a liquid solution to grow epitaxial layers on substrates.</p></p>
