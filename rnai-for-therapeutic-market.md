<p><p>RNAi (RNA interference) technology has given rise to several types of therapeutic markets, including siRNA, miRNA, and shRNA. siRNA (small interfering RNA) is a double-stranded RNA molecule that targets and degrades specific mRNA, thus preventing protein synthesis. miRNA (microRNA) also regulates gene expression, but it does so by blocking translation or promoting mRNA degradation. shRNA (short hairpin RNA) is a synthetic RNA molecule that is processed into siRNA within the cell. These different RNAi approaches offer promising possibilities for targeted therapies in various diseases.</p></p>
