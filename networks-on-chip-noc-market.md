<p><p>Networks on Chip (NoC) refer to a communication paradigm in computer systems where multiple processing elements, such as processors or cores, are interconnected through a network. Bidirectional NoC (BiNoC) is a specific type of NoC where communication between processing elements can occur in both directions, allowing simultaneous data transmission and reception. Other types of NoC markets include Unidirectional NoC, in which communication is restricted to one direction, and Multi-hop NoC, where data travels through multiple network nodes before reaching its destination. These various market types cater to different system requirements and design considerations in computer architecture.</p></p>
