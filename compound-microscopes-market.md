<p><p>Vertical microscope and inverted microscope are two types of compound microscopes commonly used in various scientific and research applications. </p><p>A vertical microscope is designed with the light source and objective lens situated below the specimen stage. This configuration allows for easy manipulation of the specimen and provides a clear field of view from above.</p><p>On the other hand, an inverted microscope has the light source and objective lens positioned above the specimen stage. This enables examination of samples that cannot be easily mounted on slides, such as living cells in a culture dish, by looking from below.</p><p>Both of these microscope types offer unique advantages depending on the specific requirements of the study or experiment, such as versatility in sample handling and ease of observation.</p></p>
