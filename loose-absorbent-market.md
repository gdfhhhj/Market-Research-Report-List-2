<p><p>The loose absorbent market consists of two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic absorbents are made from natural materials such as plant fibers or recycled paper products. They are highly absorbent and environmentally friendly, often used in spill clean-ups in sensitive areas. In contrast, inorganic absorbents are made from materials like clay or diatomaceous earth, offering high absorption capacity. They are commonly used in industrial settings to absorb liquids like oil or chemicals. Both types cater to different needs, with organic absorbents prioritizing sustainability and inorganic absorbents focusing on efficient absorption.</p></p>
