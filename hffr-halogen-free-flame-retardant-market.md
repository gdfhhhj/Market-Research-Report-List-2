Aluminum Hydroxide acts as a flame retardant by releasing water vapor, which dilutes flammable gases and reduces the rate of combustion. Phosphorus-Based flame retardants release phosphorus compounds that create a protective char layer, preventing further combustion. The "Others" category consists of alternative flame retardants like intumescent agents, which expand when exposed to heat, creating a barrier that delays ignition. These different types of HFFRs offer diverse options for reducing fire hazards in various industries.</p></p>
