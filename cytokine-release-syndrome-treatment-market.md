<p><p>Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS) treatment refers to the management and control of the potentially life-threatening immune response that occurs as a side effect of certain immunotherapies, such as CAR-T cell therapy. CRS is characterized by the excessive release of cytokines, which leads to fever, flu-like symptoms, respiratory distress, organ dysfunction, and in severe cases, death.</p><p>The primary treatment strategy for CRS involves the administration of immunosuppressive drugs, such as corticosteroids, tocilizumab, and siltuximab, to dampen the immune response and reduce inflammation. These drugs help to control the symptoms associated with CRS and prevent further complications. In addition, supportive care measures are provided, including fever control, fluid management, and monitoring of vital signs.</p><p>The global cytokine release syndrome treatment market is anticipated to witness substantial growth during the forecast period.
