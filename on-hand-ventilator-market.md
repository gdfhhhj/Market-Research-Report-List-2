<p><p>An on-hand ventilator is a respiratory device that assists patients in breathing by delivering oxygen into the lungs and expelling carbon dioxide. It is a crucial medical equipment used in critical care settings such as hospitals, ambulances, and home healthcare. On-hand ventilators are designed to provide continuous ventilation support to patients with respiratory illnesses, injuries, or those undergoing surgeries.</p><p>The market for on-hand ventilators has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is projected to continue growing at a CAGR of 5% during the forecast period. Several factors drive this growth, including the rising prevalence of respiratory diseases such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, and pneumonia. Increasing cases of COVID-19 have further amplified the demand for these devices. The pandemic has highlighted the importance of ventilators in saving lives, prompting governments and healthcare institutions to invest in expanding their inventory.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in on-hand ventilators have enhanced their performance, safety features, and portability, making them more accessible and user-friendly. Manufacturers are focusing on developing innovative products that offer improved patient comfort and better integration with other medical devices.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing preference for portable and lightweight ventilators, especially for home healthcare settings, as they provide increased mobility and convenience. Additionally, the market is witnessing a shift towards non-invasive ventilation techniques, such as bilevel positive airway pressure (BiPAP) and continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP), which offer less invasive and more comfortable treatment options.</p><p>In conclusion, the on-hand ventilator market is experiencing steady growth due to the increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases and the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.
