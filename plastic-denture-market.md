<p><p>Plastic dentures, also known as acrylic dentures, are removable dental prosthetics that are used to replace missing teeth. They are made from a type of plastic called polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), which is durable, lightweight, and easy to shape. These dentures are custom-made to fit the patient's mouth and can be easily adjusted or repaired if necessary.</p><p>The plastic denture market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. The rising prevalence of dental disorders and the increasing geriatric population, who are more prone to tooth loss, have been major factors driving the demand for plastic dentures. Additionally, the growing awareness about oral healthcare and the increasing number of dental clinics and laboratories have also contributed to market growth.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in dental implant technology and the introduction of CAD/CAM systems for denture fabrication have further boosted the market. These technological advancements have led to improved aesthetics, comfort, and durability of plastic dentures, thereby increasing their adoption.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market has witnessed a shift towards metal-free dentures as they are considered to be more biocompatible and aesthetically pleasing. Furthermore, there is a growing demand for flexible plastic dentures, which are more comfortable, lightweight, and offer better retention compared to conventional acrylic dentures.</p><p>Overall, the plastic denture market is expected to continue its growth trajectory in the coming years. According to the provided information, the market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 14.4% during the forecast period. Factors such as the rising incidence of dental disorders, the aging population, and technological advancements are anticipated to fuel market growth. Moreover, increasing dental tourism in emerging economies is also likely to contribute to the expansion of the plastic denture market.</p></p>

<p><p>The plastic denture market is divided into two types, partial dentures and full dentures. Partial dentures are used when only a few teeth are missing and they are designed to fill in the gaps. They are made from plastic and are attached to the remaining natural teeth for support. Full dentures, on the other hand, are used when all teeth are missing. They are also made from plastic and designed to replace the entire set of teeth. Both types of dentures are common solutions for individuals who have lost some or all of their natural teeth.</p></p>
