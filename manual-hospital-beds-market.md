<p><p>Manual hospital beds are specially designed beds used in hospitals and other healthcare facilities to provide support and comfort to patients during their stay. One of the key drivers is the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases and the growing geriatric population, which has led to higher demand for hospital care. Manual beds are cost-effective alternatives to electric beds, making them more accessible to hospitals and long-term care facilities with budget constraints. Moreover, these beds require minimal maintenance and can be operated even during power outages, further contributing to their popularity.</p><p>In addition, advancements in design and technology have led to the development of manual beds with improved ergonomic features, enhanced durability, and infection control mechanisms. <p><p>The manual hospital beds market consists of different types, including the single shake bed and the double shake bed. Single shake beds allow users to adjust the bed's head and foot sections individually. This enables patients to find a comfortable position. On the other hand, double shake beds go a step further by providing additional section adjustments for the knees and thighs. These beds offer enhanced flexibility and support for patients with specific needs, improving their comfort and overall hospital experience.</p></p>
