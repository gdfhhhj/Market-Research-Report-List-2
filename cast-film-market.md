<p><p>Cast film refers to a type of plastic film produced by an extrusion process, where molten plastic is forced through a flat die and cooled rapidly to form a thin, continuous sheet. The rising demand for flexible packaging solutions in industries such as food and beverage, healthcare, and consumer goods is a major driver for market growth. <p><p>Cast film is a popular packaging material available in different types. CPP (cast polypropylene) films have excellent clarity, high tensile strength, and moisture barrier properties, making them suitable for food packaging. CPE (cast polyethylene) films provide robustness, puncture resistance, and elasticity, making them ideal for applications like agriculture and hygiene products. Other types of cast films include cast PVC films, EVA films, and PET films, each with their own specific properties and applications. These films are widely used in industries such as packaging, agriculture, construction, and medical for various purposes based on their distinct characteristics.</p></p>
