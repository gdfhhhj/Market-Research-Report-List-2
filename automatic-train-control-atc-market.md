<p><p>Automatic Train Control (ATC) is a system used in rail transportation to provide continuous and automated control of trains. There are three types of ATC market: semi-automatic train operation, driverless train operation, and unattended train operation. In semi-automatic train operation, the train is controlled by both the driver and the ATC system, allowing for increased safety and efficiency. Driverless train operation eliminates the need for a human driver, relying solely on the ATC system. Unattended train operation goes a step further by removing the need for on-board personnel altogether, allowing for unmanned train operation.</p></p>
