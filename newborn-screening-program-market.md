<p><p>Newborn screening program refers to a set of tests conducted shortly after birth to identify certain genetic, metabolic, and hormonal disorders in newborns. The purpose of these screening tests is to identify conditions that may not be noticeable at birth but can lead to significant health problems if left untreated. Early detection and intervention can help prevent severe disabilities and even death in affected infants.</p><p>The global newborn screening program market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. The market is primarily driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of various genetic and metabolic disorders in newborns, rising awareness among parents and healthcare professionals about the benefits of newborn screening, and advancements in technology that have led to the development of more efficient screening methods.</p><p>Moreover, the growing government initiatives to promote newborn screening programs, along with the mandatory implementation of such programs in many countries, are further fueling market growth. For instance, in the United States, the Newborn Screening Saves Lives Reauthorization Act of 2014 provides funding to states for expanding and improving their newborn screening programs.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing preference for point-of-care testing, which allows for immediate screening and quick results, eliminating the need for sending samples to laboratories.
