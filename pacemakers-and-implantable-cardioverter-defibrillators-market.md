<p><p>Pacemakers and Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillators (ICDs) are medical devices used to treat cardiac arrhythmias and manage heart failure. Pacemakers are primarily used to regulate the heart's electrical activity by sending electrical impulses to maintain a regular heartbeat. On the other hand, ICDs continuously monitor the heart's rhythm and deliver electrical shocks when life-threatening arrhythmias are detected.</p><p>The Pacemakers and ICDs market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, such as arrhythmias and heart failure, drives the demand for these devices. According to the World Health Organization, cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death worldwide. Secondly, technological advancements in pacemakers and ICDs, such as miniaturization and improved battery life, have made these devices more efficient and reliable. Additionally, the growing geriatric population, which is more susceptible to cardiac ailments, contributes to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing various trends that are expected to shape its growth. One such trend is the shift towards leadless pacemakers, which eliminate the need for lead wires and reduce the risk of complications, such as infections and lead fractures.
