These substitutes aim to replicate the taste, appearance, and texture of caviar at a more affordable price point.</p><p>The caviar and caviar substitute market has experienced significant growth in recent years. <p><strong>The Caviar and Caviar Substitute Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Salted Processing</li><li>Pressed Processing</li><li>Pasteurized Processing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The caviar market is classified into three types based on the processing method: salted processing, pressed processing, and pasteurized processing. Salted processing involves curing the fresh caviar with salt, which enhances the taste and extends its shelf life. Pressed processing involves tightly packing the caviar to remove excess moisture, resulting in a smoother texture. Pasteurized processing involves subjecting the caviar to heat treatment to eliminate any potential bacteria while maintaining its flavor. These methods aim to preserve the quality and taste of caviar and offer consumers different options to choose from.</p></p>
