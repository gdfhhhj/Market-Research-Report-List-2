<p><p>The multifocal intraocular lens market is divided into two main types: refractive and diffraction. Refractive lenses work by bending the incoming light rays to provide clear vision at varying distances. These lenses have multiple focal points that allow users to see objects up close, intermediate, and far away without relying on glasses or contact lenses. On the other hand, diffraction lenses use microscopic rings on their surface to split light into different focal points, providing a range of vision for various distances. Both types aim to improve visual acuity and reduce dependency on external aids.</p></p>
