<p><p>Immune repertoire sequencing (IRS) is a technique used to analyze the diversity and composition of immune repertoires, which are the vast arrays of immune cells found within an organism. IRS provides a comprehensive understanding of the immune system and its response to various diseases and infections by analyzing the complete repertoire of antigen receptors present on immune cells. This high-throughput sequencing technique enables the identification and quantification of immune cell populations, their clonal expansions, and the diversity of their antigen receptors.</p><p>The immune repertoire sequencing market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing demand for personalized medicine and immune-based therapies. <p><p>Immune repertoire sequencing is a technique used to analyze the diversity of immune cells in an individual's immune system. Pyro-sequencing, also known as 454 sequencing, is a type of immune repertoire sequencing that uses the measurement of light emitted during DNA synthesis to determine the sequence of nucleotides. Dye-terminating sequencing, also called Sanger sequencing, employs chain-terminating dideoxynucleotides to determine the nucleotide sequence. Other types of immune repertoire sequencing technologies include next-generation sequencing and single-cell sequencing, which offer faster and more comprehensive analysis of immune cell diversity.</p></p>
