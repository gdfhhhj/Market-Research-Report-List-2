<p><p>Night blindness, also known as nyctalopia, is a condition characterized by poor vision in low-light conditions or at night. It is often caused by a deficiency of Vitamin A. Treatment for night blindness primarily involves addressing the underlying cause and increasing Vitamin A intake.</p><p>Vitamin A supplementation is a common treatment for night blindness. It can be administered in the form of oral supplements or injections, depending on the severity of the condition. A balanced diet rich in Vitamin A sources such as carrots, spinach, and sweet potatoes is also recommended.</p><p>In severe cases, where the underlying cause is not Vitamin A deficiency, alternative treatments may be required. These may include addressing underlying eye conditions such as cataracts, glaucoma, or retinitis pigmentosa, which could be causing the night blindness.</p><p>The night blindness treatment market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
