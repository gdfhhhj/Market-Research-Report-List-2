<p><p>Contactless payment terminals can be divided into various types based on the technology they use. Infrared terminals transmit data using infrared light, whereas Near Field Communication (NFC) terminals use short-range wireless communication technology. Bluetooth terminals establish connections through Bluetooth protocols, while Radio-frequency Identification (RFID) terminals use electromagnetic fields to identify and track tags attached to objects. Carrier-based mobile terminals rely on mobile network operators to enable payments using smartphones. These different types cater to diverse needs and preferences, providing consumers and businesses with various options for contactless payments.</p></p>
