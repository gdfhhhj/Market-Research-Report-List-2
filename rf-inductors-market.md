<p><p>RF inductors are electronic components used in radio frequency circuits to store energy in the form of magnetic fields. The market for RF inductors consists of three main types: wire wound RF inductors, film RF inductors, and multilayer RF inductors. Wire wound RF inductors are made by winding a wire around a core, providing high inductance values and low losses. Film RF inductors use a thin film to create inductance, offering compact size and high-frequency performance. Multilayer RF inductors are made by stacking multiple layers of conductor and insulating material, providing high miniaturization and good performance at high frequencies.</p></p>
