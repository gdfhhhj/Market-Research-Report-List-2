<p><p>Furandicarboxylic Acid (FDCA) is a key chemical used for the production of bioplastics. There are four main types of FDCA market processes: dehydration of hexose derivatives, oxidation of 2,5-disubstituted furans, catalytic conversions of various furan derivatives, and biological conversion of HMF. The dehydration process involves converting sugars to furan derivatives, while oxidation converts furans to produce FDCA. Catalytic conversions involve using a catalyst to transform different furan derivatives into FDCA. Lastly, biological conversion uses microorganisms to convert a compound called HMF into FDCA. These processes play a vital role in the FDCA market for the manufacturing of sustainable plastic materials.</p></p>
