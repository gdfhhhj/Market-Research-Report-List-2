<p><p>Biosimilar testing and development services market consists of various types, including effector binding, ELISA, kinetic binding assays, ADCC (antibody-dependent cell-mediated cytotoxicity), and CDC (complement-dependent cytotoxicity) cell-based assays. Effector binding tests measure the interaction between the biosimilar and its target. ELISA detects and quantifies specific proteins. Kinetic binding assays evaluate the binding kinetics of the biosimilar. ADCC and CDC cell-based assays determine the biosimilar's ability to induce antibody-dependent or complement-dependent cell-mediated cytotoxicity. These tests play a crucial role in assessing the similarity and efficacy of biosimilars during the development process.</p></p>

<p><p>Biosimilar testing and development services refer to the evaluation and analysis of biological drugs that are highly similar to existing approved drugs. The PK assay, or pharmacokinetic assay, measures how a drug is absorbed, distributed, metabolized, and excreted in the body. Tiered immunogenicity testing assesses the immune response to the drug, ensuring it does not trigger an excessive immune reaction. NAb assays, or neutralizing antibody assays, determine if the drug elicits neutralizing antibodies that may interfere with its effectiveness. These assays help ensure the safety and efficacy of biosimilar drugs before they are approved for use.</p></p>
